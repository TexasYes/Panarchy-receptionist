# Panarchy Brand Guidelines

**Source:** PANARCHY_Brand_Guidelines_2022.pdf — January 2022

---

## Brand Identity

Panarchy is the world's first **network design lab** — a firm focused on network design, combining the insights of network science, transformational leadership, and digital capability. The brand communicates intelligence, interconnection, and the future of networked organizations.

**Tagline context:** "Because Networks Are Our Nature"

---

## Logo

### Versions
| Version | Usage |
|---|---|
| **Color – Horizontal** | Primary. Use whenever possible. |
| **Color – Vertical** | When horizontal space is limited. |
| **Reversed Color** | On solid dark backgrounds or dark photographs. Preferred over white logo. |
| **White** | On black or dark Panarchy-color backgrounds when reversed color isn't possible. |
| **Black** | Only for black-and-white print. Never use when 4-color is an option. |

### Logo Colors
- **Red:** RGB 239, 62, 37 / CMYK 0, 91, 98, 0 / HEX `#EF4124`
- **Black:** RGB 6, 9, 8 / CMYK 75, 65, 67, 90 / HEX `#000000`
- **White (reversed):** RGB 255, 255, 255 / CMYK 0, 0, 0, 0

### Logo Rules
- Never appear in colors outside this guide
- Never distort the logo
- Never substitute logo fonts
- Never use the all-black logo when color is available

### Logo Mark
The bird mark (geometric/origami-style bird) can be used standalone.
- **Color:** RGB 239, 62, 37 / CMYK 0, 91, 98, 0
- **Black:** 1-color black version available for mono print

## Imagery

### Direction
Photography should reflect the nature of **network design** through rich, dark tones of **black, red, and occasional blue**.

### Subject Categories
- Abstract network/data visualizations (nodes, connections, mesh patterns)
- Neural/brain imagery
- Technology and digital abstractions (fiber optics, light patterns)
- Dark, moody atmospheric photography
- People in professional/tech environments (dark-toned, dramatic lighting)
- Cloud and sky imagery in dark/monochromatic tones

### Imagery Rules
- Favor dark backgrounds (black, near-black)
- Red network overlays and connection graphics are strongly on-brand
- Avoid bright, clean stock photography or light backgrounds
- Blue-tinted imagery (cyan/teal tones) is acceptable as accent

## Graphic Elements

### Bar Graphic
A signature Panarchy design element — a horizontal bar made up of the `network_design_white.ai` pattern layered over Panarchy Red and Panarchy Black at 20% opacity.

**Uses:**
- Highlight important facts
- Accentuate borders or page edges
- Can be used in full-width or partial-width lengths

### Single Bar / Sidebar Accent
A slim vertical red bar used to:
- Highlight text blocks or pull quotes
- Anchor corners or edges of a page
- Set off charts or callouts

## Icons

Panarchy uses a consistent line-icon library.

**Principles:**
- Functional and expressive — suitable for digital and print
- Simple and direct — never overly complicated
- Legibility at small sizes is critical
- Reduce to only necessary information
- Available in light and dark (white-on-black) versions

**Icon categories include:** AI/brain, analytics/charts, global/network, settings/gears, user/search, messaging, strategy, fingerprint/identity, teams, idea/innovation, checklist, rotation/cycle, pie chart, trophy/achievement, screen/display.

## Design System

### Background Treatments
- **Primary:** Black or near-black with red and white elements
- **Light mode:** White with black text, red accents
- **Dark mode:** Black with white text, red accents, optional blue secondary

### Layout Patterns (from print/digital examples)
- Bold white headline over dark photographic background + red accent bar
- Two-column layouts: dark image panel left, content right
- Red callout text on dark background for emphasis
- Red-on-black full-bleed hero sections
- Minimal white space layouts with strong typographic hierarchy

### Slide/Presentation Style
- Dark backgrounds preferred (black with network imagery)
- Red vertical accent bar as a divider element
- White wordmark + colored bird mark on dark slides
- Section dividers use split layout: dark photo left / light content right with red bar
- Footer: "Panarchy | Confidential | [Year]"

## Voice & Positioning (from print examples)

Panarchy communicates as a **thought leader** in network science and organizational transformation. Tone is:
- **Authoritative** — expert in network design, not a generalist
- **Forward-looking** — focused on the Network Age, the future of organizations
- **Confident** — direct statements, not hedged language
- **Intellectual** — references systems thinking, complexity science

Key themes: network design, network economy, ecosystems, adaptive organizations, panarchic risk, digital transformation.
